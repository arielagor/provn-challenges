## ICP, adapted from Meridian's tech ICP

Meridian's tech ICP is VP or Director of Sales/RevOps at a 50-500 rep software company, feeling two pains: forecast error and the inability to coach reps at scale from pipeline data. That translates into professional services with one big swap and one small one.

The big swap: "reps" become "partners, principals, and senior managers who originate and close work." A consulting firm does not have a sales team in the traditional sense. It has a partner group whose forecast is the sum of their individual pursuits. The pain is the same, just with fancier titles. The VP of Sales equivalent is the **Head of Revenue, Chief Growth Officer, or Head of Sales Enablement/Practice Excellence**. Where those titles do not exist, it is the **Managing Partner who owns the P&L**, or the **COO** when the firm has professionalized operations.

The small swap: "forecast inaccuracy" in consulting shows up as slipped bookings, proposals that stall between verbal-win and signed SOW, and partner sandbagging at quarter-end. "Rep coaching at scale" shows up as the partner-track bottleneck: senior managers who close work get promoted, senior managers who do not get pushed out, and nobody has pipeline data to tell the difference until it is too late.

**Adapted ICP:**
- 200-2,000 employees (matches the 50-500 rep band, once you count billable seniors)
- Management consulting, IT services with an advisory arm, or sector-specialist boutique
- Has a named **Head of Revenue / Growth / Sales Enablement / Practice Excellence** role, OR has a COO who owns commercial ops, OR a Managing Partner public about revenue targets
- Deal size fit: firms with $80M-$500M revenue. Below that, a $45K platform is too big a bite. Above that, the buying committee gets enterprise-sized and blows past the 60-90 day cycle.

**Signal-based qualifiers** (this is how the list gets cut from a wide universe down to 8):
1. **New Head of Revenue or Practice Excellence hired in the last 12 months.** New executives buy tools. They were hired to fix something; pipeline visibility is usually in the job description.
2. **Recent PE investment or majority recap in the last 24 months.** PE firms push portfolio companies to professionalize sales ops within the first year. Forecast discipline is the first KPI the PE partner asks about.
3. **Practice area expansion announced in press in the last 18 months.** New practice = partners from outside the firm = new pursuits with no historical baseline = forecast pain.
4. **Job posts for "Revenue Operations," "Sales Operations," or "CRM Manager."** Means they are building the function, which means they are about to buy tools.

Any firm hitting **two or more** of these signals goes on the list. Hitting three goes to the top.

## Prioritized target account list

**Note on sourcing:** Every account below was picked from public sources only: company websites, LinkedIn company pages and employee title scans, press releases, announcements, and job boards (LinkedIn Jobs, Indeed, company careers pages). No paid databases.

### 1. West Monroe Partners (TOP)

- **Signal 2 (PE pressure):** Took a recapitalization from MSD Partners a few years back and has been public about growth targets and acquisitions since. PE-backed consultancies are the single most consistent buyer of forecast-discipline tooling.
- **Signal 3 (practice expansion):** Has announced multiple practice area expansions, including a product-management and AI-enablement practice. New practice = new pursuits = no baseline = forecast pain.
- **Why #1 over #3:** Hits three signals instead of two. Already has a public Head-of-Sales-Enablement function, so the buying committee is legible from the outside. PE pressure gives us a deadline the prospect has already internalized; we do not have to manufacture urgency.

### 2. Slalom

## Accounts considered and excluded

- **Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG, Accenture (Big Four and adjacent).** Deal size and cycle mismatch. Global firms buy forecast tooling through a central procurement process that runs 9-18 months and lands as $1M+ enterprise deals. Our $45K-$120K ACV and 60-90 day cycle are exactly wrong for this profile. Also, the spec explicitly forbids Big Four at #1. We honor the spec.
- **McKinsey, BCG, Bain (MBB).** Same reason. MBB firms already have purpose-built internal tools and have historically been the hardest sell in the advisory sector for any external sales-tech platform. Not worth the pursuit cost from a cold start.
- **Oliver Wyman.** MBB-adjacent (owned by Marsh McLennan). Enterprise procurement, wrong cycle length.
- **EY-Parthenon.** Subsidiary of EY, inherits Big Four procurement. Excluded for the same reason as the parent.
- **AlixPartners.** Turnaround and restructuring specialist. Good size fit, but the firm's work itself is countercyclical, which makes pipeline forecasting less of a pain point. The pipeline for turnaround work is whatever the macro handed you this quarter.
- **Boston Consulting Group Digital Ventures.** MBB-adjacent. Excluded.

## Ranking rationale, stated plainly

Three signals beats two. Named buyer beats inferred buyer. PE deadline beats no-deadline. West Monroe wins on all three. Slalom is #2 because the buyer is legible but the firm is above our band and we would have to win a practice first. Point B is #3 because the signals are real but the employee-owned governance slows the cycle.

The #1 choice is the one where a teammate can open LinkedIn today, find the Head of Sales Enablement inside 90 seconds, and have the signal context to write a credible opening line. That is West Monroe.

## Pipeline & CRM Discipline

A teammate picking this up should know exactly where each of the 8 accounts sits, how to log every touch, what to forecast over the next 60 days, and which risks to actively defuse. The five subsections below are the operating layer of the brief.

### 1. Pipeline stage definitions

Applied to every BDR-owned account. The stages use Meridian's actual sales-cycle language, adapted to a consulting buyer.

- **S0 Target.** Account is on the list, no contact attempted yet. All 8 accounts sit here at day 0.
- **S1 Worked.** Outbound sequence is active. Touch 1 (cold email) has been sent. No response yet.
- **S2 Engaged.** Prospect showed a weak positive signal: opened the email, clicked a link, viewed the LinkedIn profile back, or accepted the LinkedIn connection without replying.
- **S3 Meeting Booked.** Discovery call is on the calendar.
- **S4 Discovered.** Discovery call completed. Pain is qualified, champion or decision-maker is named, budget authority is at least implied. SDR proposes handoff to AE.
- **S5 AE-Owned.** AE is in the primary seat. BDR role drops to supporting research and calendar coordination.
- **Closed-lost variants:** No-response after 3 touches (cooldown 30 days, re-enter at S0 with fresh signal), Disqualified (wrong fit, out of band), Not-now (defer 90 days, revisit when a new signal fires).

### 4. Deal-risk register with de-risk actions

Four named risks that apply across the full 8-account list. Each has a concrete mitigation the BDR or AE can execute without escalation.

1. **Partner-track gatekeepers.** Consultancies buy slowly because every procurement gets filtered through a partner vote. A Head of Sales Enablement can say yes to a call but cannot say yes to a contract.
   - **De-risk:** Identify an early champion below partner level (Director of Sales Ops, Head of Revenue Enablement, RevOps Manager) who will push internally. Confirm the champion during discovery, not after. Add a "champion identified yes/no" field to the S4 handoff checklist.

2. **Ambiguous ROI.** Consulting firms measure forecast accuracy differently from tech companies. A 15-25% lift in forecast accuracy that a RevOps leader at a software company buys on sight is a softer story in consulting because the denominator is a partner's gut feel, not a system number.
   - **De-risk:** Borrow Meridian's existing tech-sector case studies that quantify forecast-accuracy lift, then reframe the metric as "pursuits at risk of slipping per quarter, flagged early enough to intervene." Lead with reduced slippage on specific pursuits, not percentage lift on an abstract forecast.

3. **Pilot pricing pressure.** Consulting procurement will push for a free pilot, usually 90 days. A free pilot compresses urgency and trains the buyer to undervalue the product.
   - **De-risk:** Offer a paid 90-day proof cohort at 25% of ACV rather than free. Anchors value, filters tire-kickers, and converts to full ACV at day 91. Script the objection response in advance so the AE does not improvise on the first call.

4. **Signal decay.** The hiring posts, PE news, and practice-expansion announcements used to qualify each account are time-sensitive. A signal from 14 months ago is not a live signal; it is a historical note. Cold-emailing on stale signals is worse than not emailing at all because it marks the BDR as someone who does not read carefully.
   - **De-risk:** Monthly re-scan of public sources (LinkedIn Jobs, press releases, company news) for each qualified account. Log a `signal_as_of_date` field in the activity log. Any signal older than 90 days gets reverified before the next touch, or the account is routed back to research before it is contacted again.

### 5. Handoff trigger

BDR hands to AE at **S4 Discovered**. The handoff is gated on all three of the following, documented in the activity log before the meeting is scheduled:

1. Prospect has confirmed a named business problem that maps to a Meridian pain (forecast slippage, partner-track bottleneck, post-expansion baseline gap, post-merger forecast fog).
2. A decision-maker or internal champion has been identified by name and role.
3. Budget authority is at least implied, either by the champion confirming they own or can access a line item, or by the account being in a PE-backed or public-company profile where the procurement path is legible.

If any one of the three is missing at the end of discovery, the account stays with the BDR and gets a second discovery call rather than a premature AE handoff. Premature handoffs are the single largest cause of stalled cycles in the 60-90 day band, so the BDR's job is to hold the line on the gate.
