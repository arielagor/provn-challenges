# Challenge 88. Video Walkthrough Script (~3 min spoken)

Spoken prose. Conversational. Read as if talking to a hiring manager across a coffee table. No stage directions.

---

The top account is West Monroe Partners. Chicago-based, about 2,000 people, business and technology consulting. I put them at number one because they hit three signals where most of my list only hit two. They have publicly posted revenue operations and sales enablement roles in the last eighteen months, which is the leading indicator that they are building the function we sell into. They took a recapitalization from MSD Partners a while back and have been public about growth targets ever since, which means there is a PE partner somewhere asking about forecast discipline every quarter. And they announced a new AI enablement practice, which is the specific moment when a consulting firm's forecast gets shaky, because you are rolling pursuits with no historical baseline into the master forecast.

The opening line of my cold email is: "West Monroe adding the AI enablement practice means forecasts now have to cover pursuits with no historical baseline. That is the moment pipeline data usually stops matching what partners actually feel in their gut."

I wrote it that way for two reasons. First, there is no hello. No "hope this finds you well," no "I wanted to reach out." Consulting-firm Heads of Sales Enablement get forty cold emails a day and the first sentence is the only one that earns the second. Second, the sentence names a specific public event and ties it directly to the pain I sell into, in one breath. If that sentence is wrong about their experience, they stop reading and I deserve it. If it is right, they read the next one.

Now the objection reframe. The one I hear most is "our partners have been forecasting deals their entire careers; they know their pursuits better than any algorithm." The reframe I use is: completely agree. The partners know their pursuits better than the software. We are not the partner; we are the partner's intern. The partner remembers every pursuit they personally touched. They do not remember the 140 pursuits sitting in the other twelve partners' books. Meridian reads all 140 and flags the five that have gone quiet in a way the data says is unusual. The partner still decides what to do with the flag. The reason that reframe works is that it does not argue. It agrees with the stronger version of the prospect's claim and then redirects to the narrow problem the product actually solves.

On ICP: the signal I prioritized above all others is a recent new hire into a Head of Sales Enablement, Head of Practice Excellence, or Head of Revenue role. New executives buy tools. They were hired to fix something and pipeline visibility is usually in their mandate. That signal translates directly from Meridian's tech ICP, where a newly hired VP of Sales is the single highest conversion trigger we have. The title is different, the behavior is identical. What I almost included but cut was firms going through post-merger integration. Wavestone fits the profile and they just merged with Aguirre Partners, but integration is also the moment when tool purchases get deprioritized because everyone is busy surviving the integration. I left them on the list at number six instead of the top, because the signal is real but the timing works against us.

Now the sixty-second live pitch. Cold call to the Head of Revenue at West Monroe.

Hi, is this Jane? Jane, my name is Ariel, I am with Meridian Analytics, I know this is a cold call and I will be quick. I noticed West Monroe rolled out the AI enablement practice in the last quarter or so, and the reason I am calling specifically is that the PE-backed consultancies we work with usually feel the forecast getting shaky for about two quarters after a practice launches, because the pursuits in the new practice do not have a historical baseline to compare against. Meridian sits on top of your existing Salesforce data and flags the specific pursuits where the partner-reported confidence and the behavioral signal have drifted apart. Partners do not see us, they do not log in anywhere new. The output goes to you as one extra column inside your existing dashboards. I know you have got ten seconds left before the next meeting. Would it be worth twenty minutes next Tuesday or Wednesday to walk through what this looks like for a consultancy your size? If no, I will send a one-pager and get out of your hair.

That is the walkthrough.
