# Fieldline Growth and Launch Brief

## Funnel diagnosis

Three things are dragging trial-to-paid from 18% down to 11%. Only one of them is worth fixing first, and it is activation.

Here is the math. Trial users who reach the Pipeline Health dashboard inside 72 hours convert at three times the rate of those who don't. Only 24% of trial users ever reach it. So 76% of 14,000 trial users, roughly 10,600 accounts per cohort, are sitting in a pipeline where the core "aha" moment never fires. If we move adoption of that dashboard from 24% to 40% without any other change, and if the 3x conversion ratio holds even partially on the marginal users, blended conversion snaps back toward 15%. That is the single biggest mechanical lever on the sheet.

Compare that to the other two. The 4% click-through rate on email is genuinely bad, but email is a delivery channel for the activation moment we already know is broken. Driving more clicks into a product that fails to activate 76% of users just spreads the leak. Pipestack at $79 is a real threat and will shape head-to-head deals, but only 6 of 14 evaluations actually went head-to-head last quarter. Price is not why most trial users leave. They leave because they never saw what Fieldline does.

Fix activation first. Email and competitive pricing are second-order fights that pay off more once the product proves itself in 72 hours.

## Experiment plan

**Experiment A1: First-session Pipeline Health nudge.**

Hypothesis: if we route new trial users directly into a guided Pipeline Health setup inside their first session instead of the generic welcome tour, 72-hour activation will rise from 24% to 35%+ and trial-to-paid conversion on that cohort will rise by at least 4 points.

Primary metric: percent of new trial accounts reaching a populated Pipeline Health dashboard within 72 hours.

Guardrail metric: CRM-sync error rate during onboarding. If we push users to Pipeline Health before their CRM is clean, we can spike support tickets and get worse data. We cap the experiment if that error rate climbs more than 25% above baseline.

Success threshold: activation rate of 35% or higher, trial-to-paid lift of 4+ points versus control, both at 95% confidence over a four-week window with at least 1,500 trial accounts per arm. Promote to default onboarding on hit. Kill on miss, do not iterate on the same hypothesis twice.

## ICP definition

Fieldline Coach is for the second-line sales manager at a 100 to 400 person company running a multi-touch B2B motion, five to fifteen reps under them, quota north of a million per rep per year, already using a modern CRM. The behavioral tell a rep can qualify on in conversation: this manager is running one-on-ones every week where they pull up a rep's pipeline in the CRM and talk through three or four stalled deals from memory. They are the bottleneck on coaching. They know it, their reps know it, and they will cancel a one-on-one before they cancel a customer call. Pain signals to listen for: "my reps ask the same questions every week," "I can't scale what my top rep does," "I review pipeline Friday night because it is the only quiet time I have."

Not ICP: VP Sales at a 1,000 person company with a sales enablement team already in place. Not ICP: founder-led sales at a 20 person company where the founder is the coach and does not want a tool between them and the deal.

## Positioning

Fieldline Coach is the pipeline coach that sits inside the deal, not next to it. It is for second-line sales managers who know their reps need more coaching than a human can give. Fieldline is the right home for it because Pipeline Health already sees every deal, every risk signal, and every rep behavior. Coach turns that signal into guidance in the moment the rep is stuck, not in a weekly review a week too late.

That is the differentiation from Pipestack and from standalone conversation intelligence tools. Pipestack shows you the pipeline. Coach acts on it.

## Launch messaging snapshot

Two messages for the buyer, not for our product team.

**"Stop coaching from memory."** Sales managers know what their best reps do. They cannot hold 40 deals in their head and spot where each rep is stuck this week. Coach does that work between one-on-ones so the one-on-ones get smarter.

**"Your reps get better at the deal, not at using another tool."** Coach lives inside the pipeline your reps already check every morning. No second login, no new Slack channel, no process change. It speaks up when a deal slows down, and it shuts up otherwise.

Supporting proof point for sales to use in evaluations: Pipeline Health users already convert three times better. Coach makes Pipeline Health work for the 76% of teams who never made it there on their own.

## What this brief is deliberately not solving

It is not solving the Pipestack price delta directly. A $41 per seat gap is real, but we do not win that fight on price. We win it by making the first 72 hours of Fieldline show value that Pipestack cannot match, which is what Coach on top of Pipeline Health is actually for. If we chase price first we give up the thing we are best at.
