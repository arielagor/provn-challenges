# Challenge 89 Video Script (approximately 3 minutes)

## 1. Opening (about 20 seconds)

Vantara has a $340K three-year deal in front of Meridian Industrial. Champion is VP Procurement Dana Reyes, economic buyer is CFO Marcus Webb, blocker is VP IT Priya Nair. 27 business days to signature or the deal slips to next fiscal year. The most important thing in the next 10 business days is landing a three-way call with Dana and Marcus this week, because Dana has told me not to call Marcus directly and that handoff has been stalled for two weeks. If I do not break that stall by Friday, the math on fiscal close stops working.

## 2. Executive Brief walkthrough (about 55 seconds)

The brief is addressed to Marcus and leads with one number: Dana's eight-person team has saved 15 hours per week per person in the pilot, which translates to roughly $936,000 of recovered capacity annualized against a $340K ACV. I led with that number because Marcus has attended one demo and ducked two follow-ups. He does not need another product explanation. He needs a CFO-legible ROI in the first line.

Then I address his 12 percent discretionary spend target head-on, because dodging it is what most AE emails do and it is why they get ignored. I tell him Vantara replaces three procurement tools, which puts the line item inside his 12 percent target on consolidation alone, before the labor savings show up.

I ask for two specific things. 15 minutes on his calendar next Tuesday to read the ROI model together, with his finance partner in the room. And a separate 10 minutes to approve Priya joining the accelerated InfoSec review. Both asks are timed. Both have hard deadlines tied to fiscal close.

## 3. Deal and competitive strategy (about 55 seconds)

Close plan is week by week across 27 business days. Week one is the two binary gates: three-way call with Dana and Marcus, and InfoSec scheduled with Priya. If either slips past day five, the deal is gone.

Coupa is the competitive shadow. They have a "strategic partnership" meeting with Meridian's CPO next week. I am not trying to beat Coupa on features. I am trying to control frame. I am asking Dana for a warm intro to the CPO this week, positioned as a pilot readout, not a sales meeting. Data in the room first, from a Meridian voice, before Coupa frames the category.

On Marcus's budget objection, if he proposes one year or fewer seats, I do not discount. I reframe. A one-year contract is a churn bet he does not want to make heading into a tighter board cycle. Three years caps software inflation at today's rate. If he wants optionality on seats, we add a twelve-month utilization review, not a price concession.

Most confident about the ROI math. The pilot numbers are Dana's, not mine, and they are defensible in front of a CFO. Biggest risk is Dana's two-week stall on the Marcus handoff, because the number does not matter if it never gets into his inbox.

## 4. AI pushback moment (about 40 seconds)

The moment I pushed back hardest on the AI was on the budget objection response. Claude's first draft included a fallback offer of a term discount if Marcus pushed on the three-year. I rejected it. Discounting on a three-year-to-one-year pushback teaches the CFO that the original price was soft. It hands him a second cut before he has finished asking for the first one. And the challenge spec said explicitly, do not default to discounting.

What I did instead was rewrite the reframe so that the one-year option sounds riskier to Marcus than the three-year, not cheaper. I called it a churn bet. I tied it to his board scrutiny cycle. And I replaced the discount fallback with a twelve-month seat utilization review, which gives Marcus the optionality he actually wants without touching contract value. That one swap is the difference between a deal that closes at $340K and a deal that closes at $260K and trains the customer to negotiate the same way again at renewal.

## 5. Reflection (about 15 seconds)

With more time, I would have wanted a live conversation with Priya before filing this, not an assumed offer of the accelerated review. InfoSec people do not respond well to plans made without them. The second thing I would want is a read on whether Dana's two-week silence on the Marcus handoff is caution, or something about her relationship with him I do not know yet. If it is the second one, the whole sequence changes.
